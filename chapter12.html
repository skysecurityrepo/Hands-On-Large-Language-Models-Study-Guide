4. Full Fine-Tuning vs. PEFT (Parameter-Efficient FT)
Full Fine-Tuning
Update ALL model parameters.
  • Maximum performance
  • High compute & memory
  • Slower training
  • Larger checkpoints
PEFT (e.g., LoRA, Adapters)
Update a small subset of parameters.
  • Much less compute & memory
  • Faster training
  • Smaller checkpoints
  • Slightly lower performance (usually)
⭐
PEFT methods get great results at a fraction of the cost!

Two main approaches
RLHF (PPO)
  1. 1Train Reward Model (on human preferences)
  2. 2Optimize LLM with PPO to maximize reward while staying close to original model.
Goal: Produce helpful, honest, and harmless responses.
DPO (This Chapter)
  1. 1Use reference model (frozen)
  2. 2Optimize LLM to prefer the chosen response over the rejected one — no explicit reward model needed.

12. Evaluation Methods
Word-Level Metrics
  • Perplexity: How well the model predicts text.
  • BLEU / ROUGE: N-gram overlap with references.
  • BERTScore: Semantic similarity using embeddings.
  • Limitations: Don't capture correctness, reasoning, or human preference.
Benchmarks (Examples)
  • MMLU: 57 tasks across many topics
  • GSM8K: Grade-school math
  • HumanEval: Coding tasks
  • TruthfulQA: Truthfulness
  • HellaSwag: Commonsense reasoning
  • GLUE: Language understanding
LLM-as-a-Judge
  • Use a strong LLM to score many topics.
  • Pairwise comparison is common.
  • Scales well as models improve.

16. Key Takeaways
  • Pretraining teaches language; fine-tuning teaches behavior.
  • SFT makes models follow instructions.
  • Preference tuning aligns models with human values.
  • PEFT (LoRA, QLoRA) makes fine-tuning affordable.
  • DPO is a simpler alternative to RLHF (PPO).
  • Evaluation needs multiple metrics ≠ human judgment.
  • Good data and careful tuning beat more compute.
  • Always test your model on real tasks!
