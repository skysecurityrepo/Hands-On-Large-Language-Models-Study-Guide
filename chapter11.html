5. Contrastive Learning Basics
Teach the model what is similar vs different.
Positive Pair (Similar)
Q: "How many people live in Amsterdam?"
A: "Almost a million people live in Amsterdam."
✅
Hard Negative (Dissimilar)
Q: "How many people live in Amsterdam?"
A: "More than a million people live in Utrecht."
❌
Contrastive learning pulls similar pairs together and pushes apart pairs in vector space.
6. Loss Functions Overview
Cosine Similarity Loss
(For similarity score 0 to 1)
Minimize: [cos(a, b) − label]²

  • Intuitive
  • Works with soft similarity scores
  • Good for STS tasks
Multiple Negatives Ranking Loss
(InfoNCE / NTXent)
Compare anchor to many in-batch negatives
Anchor (A) → Positive (P) + Negatives (N₁, N₂…)
  • Requires large batch
  • Strong performance
  • Most commonly used

Key Steps

  5. 5Train with Trainer

Training Arguments (Examples)
  • learning_rate = 2e-5
  • batch_size = 16 or 32
  • num_epochs = 1 – 3
  • fp16 = True
  • eval_steps = 100
  • weight_decay = 0.01

10. Few-Shot Classification with SetFit
High performance with very few labeled examples.
SetFit 3-Step Process
1️⃣
Sample Pairs
From few labeled examples, create positive (in-class) and negative (out-class) pairs.
2️⃣
Fine-Tune ST Model
Fine-tune a SentenceTransformer model using contrastive learning.
3️⃣
Train Classifier
Use embeddings to train a simple classifier (e.g., Logistic Regression).
Why SetFit?
  • Works with as few as 8–16 examples per class!
  • Much faster than full fine-tuning
  • Competitive with full fine-tuning on large data

16. Best Practices & Tips
  • Use the best pretrained model available
  • Collect high-quality labeled data
  • Use hard negatives when possible
  • Monitor metrics to avoid overfitting
  • Try different losses and batch sizes
  • Evaluate on STSB & multiple tasks
  • Save & version your models
Key Takeaways
  • Fine-tuning outperforms frozen models.
  • Contrastive learning is key to embeddings.
  • SetFit enables few-shot success.
  • MLM adapts models to your domain.
  • NER works at the token level.
  • Evaluate well — trust the metrics!
